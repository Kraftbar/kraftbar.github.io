    example: wǔ
    example: xue (xüe)
    yu, wu, ju, xu, qu

    common chars remembering rule:
    zh nor

    ü -> u
    o -> å

  1. Pictograms 象xiàng 形xíng
    2. 火 (huǒ) fire
    水 (shuǐ) water
    人 (rén) man
    木 (mù) tree
    口 (kǒu) mouth
    山 (shān) mountain

  2. Simple ideograms 指zhǐ 事shì
    3. One 一yī
    Two 二èr
    Three 三sān
    ‘up’ 上 (shàng) and ‘down’ 下 (xià)
    木 (mù), to make 末 (mò) ‘tip’ and 本 (běn) ‘root’.

  3. Ideogrammatic compounds 会huì 意yì
    4. 女 (nǚ) woman + 宀 (mián) roof = 安 (ān) safe
    手 (shǒu) hand + 目 (mù) eye = 看 (kàn) to see
    人 (rén) man ＋ 木 (mù) tree = 休 (xiū) to rest
    田 (tián) field + 木 (mù) tree = 果 (guǒ) fruit

  4. Phono-semantic compounds 形xíng 声shēng
    5. The character 木 (mù) is nearly always the ‘determinative’ in a compound word. This means that the compounds are almost always associated with wood or something wooden.
    Determinative Phonetic Compound
    水 (shuǐ) water 其 (qí) 淇 (qí) River Qi
    玉 (yù) jade 其 (qí) 琪 (qí) a valuable white stone
    土 (tǔ) earth 其 (qí) 基 (jī) foundation
    木 (mù) wood 反 (fǎn) to turn over 板 (bǎn) board
    木 (mù) wood 每 (měi) every 梅 (méi) plum
    A good example of this is the phonetic of 工 (gōng) which means ‘work’:
    Compound Pronunciation / Pinyin Meaning
    功 gōng achievement
    红 hóng red
    杠 gàng / gāng bar / pole
    扛 káng / gāng to carry high or above shoulders
    江 jiāng river

  5. Derivative cognates 转zhuǎn 注zhù
    6. 氵water
    木 mù
    沐 mù "to wash oneself"
    林 lín
    淋 lín "to pour"
    转注 (zhuǎn zhù) ‘transfer’ characters are the most difficult to explain and are the least understood. These characters could be interchangeably with each other due to them sharing the same radical and having a similar etymology.
    For example:
    爸 (bà) / 父 (fù) father
